Most packing advice is obvious. "Use packing paper. Label your boxes." Sure. But there's a lot that doesn't get said — the stuff you only learn after watching hundreds of moves go sideways.

Here are seven things that actually make a difference.

## 1. Pack Heaviest Items in the Smallest Boxes

This one seems backwards until you try to lift a large box full of books. Small boxes for heavy items (books, dishes, tools), large boxes for light items (linens, pillows, clothing). If a box is too heavy to pick up with one hand, it's too heavy to be safe.

## 2. Label Every Box on Three Sides

Labels on top get buried when boxes are stacked. Label two sides and the top. Include the room **and** a brief description — "Kitchen — pots and pans" is more useful than just "Kitchen" when you're looking for something specific at 9pm on move night.

## 3. Pack a "First Night" Box Separately

This one box travels in your car, not the truck. It holds everything you'll need in the first 24 hours: phone chargers, a change of clothes, toiletries, coffee setup, medications, important documents. Everything else can wait. This box doesn't.

## 4. Don't Leave Drawers Empty

Most furniture pieces can be moved with clothes and soft items still in the drawers — it saves boxes and unpacking time. Ask your crew first, but for dressers and filing cabinets, this often works well.

## 5. Use Wardrobe Boxes for Hanging Clothes

If you have significant hanging wardrobe items, wardrobe boxes let them stay on hangers during the move. No folding, no wrinkling, no unpacking step — they go straight from the old closet to the new one.

## 6. Photograph Cable Setups Before Disconnecting

Before you unplug your TV, entertainment center, or home office setup, take a photo of all the cables. You'll thank yourself when setup day comes. Do the same for any furniture you have to disassemble.

## 7. Finish Packing One Room Before Moving to Another

It's tempting to jump around. Resist. Complete one room fully before starting the next. This makes loading the truck faster and makes unpacking at the new place dramatically less confusing.
