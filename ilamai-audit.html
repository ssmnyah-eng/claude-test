The Lead Generation Gap

1,030 followers is a viable local audience — but only if they know what to do next. Right now, an interested visitor has no frictionless path to becoming a patient. No DM prompt, no story booking link, no “reply with your question” invitation. Conversion has to be engineered into every post. That’s the gap. It’s not a content volume problem — it’s a content structure problem.

The Lead Generation Gap on Facebook

Facebook’s highest-value use case for a local med spa is not organic feed posts — it’s Facebook Lead Ads targeting local women by age and interest. Even a small spend ($300–500/month targeting Fredericksburg women 35–60 interested in weight loss or hormone therapy) can produce qualified consultation requests within days. That tool is sitting completely unused while the organic page generates almost no reach.

Action Plan
Priority Actions — In Order
Sequenced by impact. Top items remove active damage; later items build compounding growth.
1
Merge or delete the duplicate Facebook page
Two pages mean no page effectively grows. Merge into the primary page (ID: 61566798147499) and redirect all traffic there. This is the only Facebook action that matters before anything else.
2
Add a hook and CTA to every Instagram post going forward
The first line of every caption should earn the tap to “more.” The last line should give a specific next step. This alone will begin converting existing followers into consultations.
3
Replace Instagram-mirrored Facebook posts with Facebook-native content
Facebook rewards longer-form text, community questions, and shared knowledge. Reposting Instagram content actively suppresses reach. These need to be different posts entirely.
4
Build out the “Tea Time with Dr. Uma” YouTube series with SEO intent
Three to four videos on the highest-search, highest-fear topics will begin ranking locally within 60–90 days and convert viewers at a far higher rate than any social post.
5
Run one Facebook Lead Ad targeting local Weight Loss or BHRT intent
A $300 Facebook Lead Ad campaign targeting Fredericksburg-area women aged 35–60 will produce qualified consultation leads that organic content at 73 followers simply cannot.
